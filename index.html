Eye Movements: Saccades and Smooth Pursuit Animations

Human vision is driven by rapid and dynamic eye movements. Saccades are quick, ballistic jumps that redirect our gaze from one point to another, allowing us to scan scenes efficiently, accompanied by fixations which are brief pauses between saccades where the eyes remain relatively still, enabling detailed visual processing. Together, these movements are essential for scanning our environment, reading, recognizing faces, and following motion. Without them, our perception would be fragmented and unstable.
